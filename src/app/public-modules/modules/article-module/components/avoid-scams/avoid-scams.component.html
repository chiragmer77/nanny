How Au pair can avoids scams
1. Asking for Money

For most countries Au pairs do not have to pay money. But in some countries Au pairs has to pay. So first you need to research on if you have to pay anything for that particular country. In Netherlands an agency can collect a maximum of 34 Euros from an Au pair. In some countries Au pair need to pay for flight ticket. In that case do not pay money instead buy a refundable ticket by your self. For USA Au pair has to pay 1000-2000 dollar to agency. Need to make sure its a real agency

2. UK & USA Au pair Visa Scams

Au pairs get lot of mails to apply for UK visa. They even give a Fake UK Au pair application form to collect money from Au pair. The UK does not have an Au pair Visa. They have a Youth mobility visa which is applicable for Australlia, Canada, Iceland, Hong Kong, Japan South Korea and Taiwan. So if someone send a UK Visa application form and ask to pay money it will be a scam.

In USA only a recognised agency can apply for a J1 Visa. You have to check the website of the agency and google search the agency name

3. Identify Fake agencies from Email

There are Fake Au pair agencies asking for payment. Scammers can also act as employees of a real agency. There are some ways to identify if they are fake or not

  1. If an agency send you an email from gmail account it could be a fake. A real agency will never use gmail account. A real agency will have an email account with @agencyname.com and not @gmail.com
  2. If you get an email from an email adress that has agency name for example @agencyname.com then there should also be a website with that name. If a website do not exist with that name it could be a fake. If you find the website then search for that website name in google search and try to find if it has more search result and see if you can find reviews.

    For example if you get an email from info@nannyaupair.com then you can copy nannyaupair.com and check if such a website exist.

    On the right side image you can see an email that an Au pair recently got from a scammer. Scammer acts as an host mother and asking to send email to application@ukvisas-immigration.com to get a visa. There is no website with name ukvisas-immigration.com. Even if it exist you can search about it in google. You can also google search to find real website of visa office.

    Technically the last part of the email and name of website are same. When you reply also make sure you reply back to agency email and not to gmail or yahoo email.
4. Insist on a Video call

Video call is the easiest way to find if its a real family or not. Ask to have video call with full family

If a Family refuse to have a video call, then its most probably a scammer. A real family will always have a video call before inviting someone to live at their home.

5. Fake Money transfers

Most of the scammers ask to send money using MoneyGram, Western Union etc. If they say they live in Europe and ask to send money to an African Bank account it could be a scam. If you want to check for valid European bank account you can use this website - www.iban.org . But they only allow to check few numbers only.

6. Where are scammers based

Most of the scammers are based in African countries though they claim to live in Europe. If they send mail to 25 girls atleast one will pay money. They can make a lot of money if they send such email to plenty of girls. Thats how the scammers live.

Scammers can act as a host family or ex Au pair or as agency. After Au pair pays the money the scammers just disappear or block the Au pair
7. Partially Fake local agencies

In some Asian and African countries there are local agencies that help to find Au pair jobs abroad. They have facebook pages which shows the Au pairs and that they have send abroad. The thing is they collect money from everyone who register with them but only some get interview chance and only few are selected.

We got informed by an Indonesian girl that she has to pay around 4 months of her salary to register at a local agency in Indonesia and she did not get any interview call. She even quit her job to be a Au pair in Europe. That agency have placed few Au pairs in Europe and with their pictures on social media, the agency can collect money from lot of poor girls.

Avoid Facebook scams
8. Do not trust Empty Facebook Profiles
  1. Do not trust Facebook profiles that does not have much information
  2. Do not trust a recently created Facebook profile. A real profile could have been created many years ago
  3. Do not trust a Facebook profile that does not have much friends or Likes or comments
9. Identify Fake Facebook profiles
  1. Check Friend ListIf a family claims to be European or from US and all their friends in the list are from Africa or Asia there is a good chance that this account was created for scamming people. A European person should have atleast some local friends.
  2. Check Comments & LikesIf a family claims to be European or from US and the like and comments on their post are only from Africa or Asia it could be a Fake profile.

This is a Fake profile in which family is pretending to be from UK. All the likes and comments are from Asians. A real UK family will have most likes and comments from people in UK. UK dont even have a Au pair visa but still lot of Au pairs go behind this.

10. Identify Fake Posts
  1. In General most US Families that look for Au pairs in European groups are Fake.
  2. If a family turns off commenting on a post it could be a scammer. Because he does not want anyone else to comment that it is a scam.

This Fake host family Turned off commenting on post. In general USA job offer in European groups is always a scam
11. Identify Lover Boys and Human traffikers
  1. There are Guys looking for relationships in Au pair groups and Au pair websites
  2. In general they are the part of human traffiking gangs. They use girls for many illegal activities. Better dont reply to them and report them
